the story

Non-linear path.
Intentional direction.

Started in the Visible Heart Lab at the University of Minnesota — studying cardiac physiology and biomedical engineering at the device level. That foundation of understanding how complex systems fail, adapt, and heal has shaped how I think about product ever since.

Went sideways into video and brand storytelling at Motion 117, learning that the hardest part of any complex idea is making it land for someone who doesn't already care. That skill travels.

Spent time in the UMN Technology Commercialization office, helping researchers cross the gap between a working idea and a real product — market fit, regulatory strategy, financial models.

Now: senior product leader building platforms that help people navigate the hardest moments in health — from a 0→1 care navigation build that cut time-to-care to 48 hours, to a behavioral health analytics platform serving enterprise clients at national scale.

Resources for Living's EAP had a fundamental access problem: members were authorized for care but couldn't get to it fast enough. Scheduling was fragmented, provider matching was manual, and the digital experience hadn't kept pace.
  • Led discovery across clinical, operations, and account stakeholders to map the broken workflow and find the highest-leverage intervention points
  • Owned the full product roadmap — prioritizing scheduling and navigation first, then building automated engagement workflows on top
